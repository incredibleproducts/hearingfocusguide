How To Sleep Better With Tinnitus

Simple Strategies That May Help Improve Nighttime Comfort

Many adults find that tinnitus symptoms become more noticeable at night. When the environment becomes quiet, ringing or buzzing sounds may seem more intense and interfere with relaxation and sleep.

Learn More

Why Tinnitus Often Feels Worse At Night

During the day, surrounding sounds help distract the brain from internal hearing sensations. At night, when those sounds disappear, ringing, buzzing or humming noises may become much more noticeable. This can make falling asleep and staying asleep more difficult.

Common Sleep Challenges Reported By Adults

Difficulty Falling Asleep

Persistent ringing sounds may make it harder to relax before bedtime.

Frequent Night Awakenings

Some individuals wake up and become more aware of tinnitus symptoms.

Stress And Frustration

Poor sleep and hearing discomfort may create additional stress.

Daytime Fatigue

Interrupted sleep often affects energy levels the following day.

Helpful Habits Before Bed

Use Soft Background Sound

Gentle sounds such as rain, ocean waves or white noise may help mask unwanted hearing sensations.

Reduce Evening Stress

Relaxation techniques may help create a calmer bedtime routine.

Avoid Excess Stimulation

Limiting caffeine and electronic devices before bed may improve sleep quality.

Maintain Consistent Sleep Hours

A regular sleep schedule supports healthy sleep habits.

Supporting Hearing Wellness During The Day

Many adults focus not only on improving sleep habits but also on supporting overall hearing wellness through nutrition, healthy lifestyle choices and proactive self-care routines. Small daily habits may contribute to greater comfort and quality of life.
